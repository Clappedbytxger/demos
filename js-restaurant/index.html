Our Story

From a 50-seat pie house to Newberg's family table

Back in 1975, J's opened its doors as J's Family Diner and Pie House — a 50-seat family restaurant that brought a welcome new casual dining experience to Newberg. The community embraced it from day one.

Soon after opening, general manager Craig took over as owner and ran J's with great success until 1999 — adding three building expansions along the way to keep up with demand. That's how J's grew into the 250-seat family restaurant and lounge you see today.

In 1999, current owner Sean Reed and his father Jim took over and began the current legacy: always looking toward the future while honoring the past. In fact, about a quarter of today's menu is made up of crowd favorites from the original 1975 menu.

Over the last decade we've doubled down on what matters: local sourcing, no GMOs, no growth hormones, and fresh-from-scratch cooking — breads, desserts and breakfast treats baked daily, and homemade soups, always. Everything we make, we make with the best ingredient of all: love.
